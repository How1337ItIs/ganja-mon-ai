# Cannabis Growth Stage Parameters

Comprehensive environmental parameters for optimal cannabis cultivation across all growth stages.

## Overview

Cannabis plants progress through distinct growth stages, each requiring specific environmental conditions for optimal health and yield. This document provides detailed parameter ranges based on industry standards and research from sources including Grow Weed Easy, Royal Queen Seeds, CocoforCannabis, and academic literature.

---

## Growth Stages

1. **Seedling** (Weeks 1-3)
2. **Vegetative** (Weeks 3-8+)
3. **Transition/Pre-Flower** (Weeks 1-2 of flower)
4. **Flowering** (Weeks 3-8)
5. **Late Flower/Ripening** (Weeks 7-10+)
6. **Harvest/Flush** (Final 1-2 weeks)

## Detailed Stage Breakdowns

### 1. Seedling Stage (Weeks 1-3)

**Duration:** 1-3 weeks from germination

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 68-77°F (20-25°C)
- **Night:** 65-70°F (18-21°C)
- **Rationale:** Warm but not hot. Seedlings are delicate and need consistent warmth for root development.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 65-70% RH
- **Rationale:** High humidity reduces transpiration stress on young plants with underdeveloped root systems.

**VPD (Vapor Pressure Deficit):**
- **Target:** 0.4-0.6 kPa
- **Rationale:** Low VPD prevents excessive water loss through leaves while roots establish.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 18/6 (18 hours on, 6 hours off) or 24/0 for autoflowers
- **Rationale:** Long light periods encourage vegetative growth without stressing seedlings.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 200-400 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 12-20 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** Moderate intensity prevents light stress while providing sufficient energy for growth.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 400-600 ppm (ambient to slightly elevated)
- **Rationale:** Seedlings don't require CO2 enrichment; ambient levels are sufficient.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.0
- **Rationale:** Slightly acidic to neutral pH ensures nutrient availability.

**EC (Electrical Conductivity):**
- **Range:** 0.4-0.6 mS/cm
- **Rationale:** Very low nutrient concentration; seedlings need minimal feeding.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 2-3 days
- **Method:** Light misting or small amounts of water
- **Rationale:** Keep medium moist but not saturated to prevent damping off.

---

### 2. Vegetative Stage (Weeks 3-8+)

**Duration:** 3-8+ weeks (can extend indefinitely for mother plants)

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 70-82°F (21-28°C)
- **Night:** 65-72°F (18-22°C)
- **Rationale:** Warmer temperatures promote vigorous vegetative growth and metabolic activity.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 40-60% RH
- **Rationale:** Lower humidity encourages stronger root development and reduces mold risk.

**VPD:**
- **Target:** 0.8-1.2 kPa
- **Rationale:** Moderate VPD optimizes transpiration and nutrient uptake.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 18/6 (18 hours on, 6 hours off)
- **Rationale:** Long light periods maximize vegetative growth; 6-hour dark period is essential for plant health.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 400-600 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 25-35 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** Higher intensity supports rapid leaf and stem development.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 400-600 ppm (ambient)
- **Enrichment:** Can increase to 1000-1200 ppm with proper ventilation
- **Rationale:** CO2 enrichment accelerates growth but requires adequate air exchange.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.5
- **Rationale:** Maintains nutrient availability as feeding increases.

**EC:**
- **Range:** 1.2-1.8 mS/cm
- **Rationale:** Moderate nutrient levels support active growth without burning.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 1-2 days
- **Method:** Water until 10-20% runoff
- **Rationale:** Increased water needs match increased transpiration and growth.

---

### 3. Transition/Pre-Flower Stage (Weeks 1-2 of Flower)

**Duration:** First 1-2 weeks after switching to 12/12

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 68-80°F (20-27°C)
- **Night:** 65-70°F (18-21°C)
- **Rationale:** Slightly cooler than veg to signal flowering transition.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 40-50% RH
- **Rationale:** Lower humidity prepares plants for flowering and reduces mold risk.

**VPD:**
- **Target:** 1.0-1.2 kPa
- **Rationale:** Higher VPD encourages flowering hormone production.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 12/12 (12 hours on, 12 hours off)
- **Rationale:** Critical photoperiod change triggers flowering in photoperiod strains.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 600-800 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 30-40 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** Increased intensity supports flower initiation and early bud development.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 600-800 ppm
- **Rationale:** Slightly elevated CO2 supports increased metabolic demands.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.0
- **Rationale:** Maintains nutrient availability during transition.

**EC:**
- **Range:** 1.8-2.2 mS/cm
- **Rationale:** Increased nutrients support flower development.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 1-2 days
- **Method:** Water until 10-20% runoff
- **Rationale:** Consistent moisture supports rapid flower development.

---

### 4. Flowering Stage (Weeks 3-8)

**Duration:** Weeks 3-8 of flowering (peak bud development)

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 68-78°F (20-26°C)
- **Night:** 65-70°F (18-21°C)
- **Rationale:** Cooler temperatures preserve terpenes and prevent heat stress during critical bud development.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 40-50% RH
- **Critical:** Must stay below 50% to prevent bud rot
- **Rationale:** Lower humidity is essential for dense bud development and mold prevention.

**VPD:**
- **Target:** 1.0-1.2 kPa
- **Rationale:** Optimal VPD balances transpiration with mold prevention.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 12/12 (12 hours on, 12 hours off)
- **Critical:** Must maintain strict 12/12; light leaks can cause hermaphroditism
- **Rationale:** Consistent dark period is essential for flowering hormone production.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 600-900 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 30-45 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** High intensity maximizes bud density and resin production.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 800-1200 ppm (with proper ventilation)
- **Rationale:** CO2 enrichment significantly increases yield during flowering.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.0
- **Rationale:** Maintains nutrient availability, especially phosphorus and potassium.

**EC:**
- **Range:** 2.0-2.5 mS/cm
- **Rationale:** Peak nutrient demand during bud development.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 1-2 days
- **Method:** Water until 10-20% runoff
- **Rationale:** Consistent moisture supports bud swelling.

---

### 5. Late Flower/Ripening Stage (Weeks 7-10+)

**Duration:** Final 2-4 weeks before harvest

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 65-75°F (18-24°C)
- **Night:** 60-68°F (15-20°C)
- **Rationale:** Cooler temperatures enhance terpene production and preserve cannabinoids.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 35-45% RH
- **Critical:** Lower humidity is essential to prevent bud rot in dense flowers
- **Rationale:** Reduced humidity protects maturing buds and encourages resin production.

**VPD:**
- **Target:** 1.2-1.4 kPa
- **Rationale:** Higher VPD reduces mold risk while maintaining plant health.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 12/12 (maintain strict schedule)
- **Rationale:** Consistent schedule ensures proper ripening.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 600-800 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 30-40 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** Slightly reduced intensity prevents light stress on mature buds.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Rationale:** Moderate CO2 supports final bud development.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.0
- **Rationale:** Maintains nutrient availability during ripening.

**EC:**
- **Range:** 1.5-2.0 mS/cm
- **Rationale:** Reduced nutrients as plants approach harvest.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 2-3 days
- **Method:** Allow slight dry-back between waterings
- **Rationale:** Slight stress can enhance resin production and terpene content.

---

### 6. Harvest/Flush Stage (Final 1-2 weeks)

**Duration:** Last 1-2 weeks before harvest

**Temperature:**
- **Day:** 65-75°F (18-24°C)
- **Night:** 60-68°F (15-20°C)
- **Rationale:** Cool temperatures preserve terpenes and cannabinoids.

**Humidity:**
- **Range:** 30-40% RH
- **Critical:** Very low humidity prevents mold during final ripening
- **Rationale:** Lowest humidity of the cycle protects dense, mature buds.

**VPD:**
- **Target:** 1.4-1.6 kPa
- **Rationale:** Highest VPD reduces mold risk to absolute minimum.

**Light Schedule:**
- **Photoperiod:** 12/12 (maintain until harvest)
- **Rationale:** Continue until harvest day.

**Light Intensity:**
- **PPFD:** 400-600 μmol/m²/s
- **DLI:** 20-30 mol/m²/day
- **Rationale:** Reduced intensity allows plants to finish naturally.

**CO2:**
- **Range:** 400-600 ppm (ambient)
- **Rationale:** Return to ambient levels as plants finish.

**pH:**
- **Soil:** 6.0-7.0
- **Hydroponic:** 5.5-6.0
- **Rationale:** Maintain pH for final nutrient uptake.

**EC:**
- **Range:** 0.0-0.5 mS/cm (flush with plain water)
- **Rationale:** Flushing removes excess nutrients for cleaner taste.

**Watering:**
- **Frequency:** Every 2-3 days
- **Method:** Plain pH-adjusted water only (no nutrients)
- **Rationale:** Flushing removes residual nutrients from plant tissue.

## Additional Considerations

### VPD (Vapor Pressure Deficit) Chart Reference

VPD is calculated from temperature and humidity. Optimal ranges by stage:

| Stage | VPD Range (kPa) | Temp Range (°F) | Humidity Range (%) |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Seedling | 0.4-0.6 | 68-77 | 65-70 |
| Vegetative | 0.8-1.2 | 70-82 | 40-60 |
| Transition | 1.0-1.2 | 68-80 | 40-50 |
| Flowering | 1.0-1.2 | 68-78 | 40-50 |
| Late Flower | 1.2-1.4 | 65-75 | 35-45 |
| Harvest | 1.4-1.6 | 65-75 | 30-40 |

**VPD Calculation:**
```
VPD = SVP × (1 - RH/100)
Where SVP (Saturation Vapor Pressure) depends on temperature
```

### Light Intensity Guidelines

**PPFD (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density):**
- Measured in μmol/m²/s
- Use a quantum PAR meter for accurate readings
- Adjust light height to achieve target PPFD

**DLI (Daily Light Integral):**
- Total light energy per day (mol/m²/day)
- DLI = PPFD × (hours of light) × 3600 / 1,000,000

**Light Distance Guidelines:**
- **LED:** 12-24 inches (adjust for PPFD)
- **HPS:** 18-36 inches (adjust for PPFD)
- **CMH/LEC:** 18-30 inches (adjust for PPFD)

### CO2 Enrichment

**When to Use:**
- Vegetative stage: Optional (400-1200 ppm)
- Flowering stage: Recommended (800-1200 ppm)

**Requirements:**
- Sealed grow room or tent
- CO2 generator or tank with regulator
- Monitor/controller for safety
- Adequate ventilation when CO2 is off

**Safety:**
- Never exceed 1500 ppm (can be harmful to humans)
- Ensure proper ventilation during maintenance
- Use CO2 monitors with alarms

### pH Management

**Soil pH:**
- Optimal: 6.0-7.0
- Test runoff water to monitor
- Adjust with pH up/down solutions

**Hydroponic pH:**
- Optimal: 5.5-6.0 (slightly more acidic)
- Monitor daily
- Adjust as needed (pH drifts over time)

**pH Lockout:**
- Occurs when pH is outside optimal range
- Symptoms: Nutrient deficiencies despite feeding
- Solution: Flush and adjust pH

### EC/Nutrient Strength

**EC (Electrical Conductivity):**
- Measures total dissolved solids (nutrients)
- Higher EC = more nutrients
- Monitor runoff to prevent buildup

**Nutrient Schedule:**
- **Seedling:** 25% strength
- **Early Veg:** 50% strength
- **Late Veg:** 75-100% strength
- **Flower:** 100% strength (bloom nutrients)
- **Late Flower:** 75% strength
- **Flush:** 0% (plain water)

### Watering Best Practices

**Soil:**
- Water when top 1-2 inches are dry
- Water until 10-20% runoff
- Allow slight dry-back between waterings

**Hydroponic:**
- Continuous flow (DWC, NFT) or timed cycles (Ebb & Flow)
- Monitor reservoir levels daily
- Change reservoir weekly

**Signs of Overwatering:**
- Drooping leaves
- Slow growth
- Root rot
- Mold/mildew

**Signs of Underwatering:**
- Dry, crispy leaves
- Wilting
- Slow growth
- Nutrient burn (concentrated solution)

## Stage-Specific Nutrient Requirements

### Seedling
- **N-P-K:** Minimal (0.4-0.6 EC)
- **Focus:** Root development
- **Supplements:** Root stimulators (optional)

### Vegetative
- **N-P-K:** High N, moderate P, moderate K (e.g., 3-1-2)
- **Focus:** Leaf and stem growth
- **Supplements:** Cal-Mag, beneficial bacteria

### Transition
- **N-P-K:** Balanced (e.g., 2-2-2)
- **Focus:** Flower initiation
- **Supplements:** Bloom boosters begin

### Flowering
- **N-P-K:** Low N, high P, high K (e.g., 1-3-2)
- **Focus:** Bud development
- **Supplements:** PK boosters, silica

### Late Flower
- **N-P-K:** Very low N, high P, high K (e.g., 0-5-4)
- **Focus:** Bud density and resin
- **Supplements:** PK boosters, terpene enhancers

### Harvest/Flush
- **N-P-K:** None (0.0-0.5 EC)
- **Focus:** Nutrient removal
- **Supplements:** None (plain water only)

## Implementation Notes for Grok & Mon

### Sensor Targets
- **Temperature:** DHT22 or SCD30 (with temp)
- **Humidity:** DHT22 or SCD30
- **VPD:** Calculated from temp/humidity
- **Light:** Quantum PAR sensor for PPFD
- **CO2:** SCD30 or SCD40
- **pH:** pH probe (for hydro) or runoff testing (for soil)
- **EC:** EC/TDS meter
- **Soil Moisture:** Capacitive moisture sensor

### AI Decision Framework
The AI should:
1. Monitor current stage based on time since flip or autoflower genetics
2. Compare current parameters to stage targets
3. Adjust actuators (lights, fans, humidifiers, heaters) to reach targets
4. Log deviations and adjust gradually
5. Respect safety constraints (no light during dark period, etc.)

### Safety Constraints
- Never turn lights on during flowering dark period
- Temperature: 55-95°F absolute limits
- Humidity: 20-90% absolute limits
- VPD: Monitor to prevent excessive stress
- CO2: Never exceed 1500 ppm
- Irrigation: Max once per hour
