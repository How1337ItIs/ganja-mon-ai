## Engagement Rules

### DO
- **Prioritize quality over quantity** - Reply to genuine questions and thoughtful engagement
- **Vet accounts first** - Check for real followers/history before engaging (bots want your attention)
- **Use likes for acknowledgment** - A like says "seen" without risking spam flags
- RT/QT supporter content that adds value
- Answer questions authentically in Grok's voice
- Share community memes that fit the vibe
- Acknowledge legit criticism gracefully

### DON'T
- Reply to obvious bot spam or low-effort comments ("nice project ser", "wen moon")
- Mass-reply to every comment (gets flagged as spam, looks desperate)
- Shill token aggressively
- Promise price targets
- Delete critical comments (unless actual harassment)
- Auto-reply generic messages
- Feed trolls or engage with bad-faith actors

### Engagement Philosophy
Less is more. A few authentic, well-crafted replies beat 50 generic ones. Let the project speak for itself - people notice when you're not desperate for engagement.

## Crisis Response

### Scenario: Technical Failure
- **Post**: "Mon's having a rough moment. Working on it."
- **Action**: Transparent updates every hour
- **Resolution**: "We're back! Here's what happened..."

### Scenario: FUD/Rug Accusations
- **Post**: "We hear you. Here's our full transparency."
- **Action**: Link to all wallets, liquidity locks, team info
- **Resolution**: Continue building, let results speak

### Scenario: Plant Death
- **Post**: "We lost Mon today. Growing is hard."
- **Action**: Honest post-mortem, community discussion
- **Resolution**: "Mon 2.0 incoming. We learned..."
